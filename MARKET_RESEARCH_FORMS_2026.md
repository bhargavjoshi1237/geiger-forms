# Geiger Forms Market Research 2026

Research date: 2026-06-10

Goal: identify leading form products, summarize what they offer, and create a feature rarity framework so Geiger Forms can choose features deliberately instead of becoming a generic checklist clone.

## Executive Read

The forms market has split into several lanes:

1. Simple collection tools: Google Forms and Microsoft Forms win because they are free or bundled, familiar, and good enough for internal surveys, quizzes, and lightweight data collection.
2. Broad no-code form suites: Jotform, Paperform, Zoho Forms, Cognito Forms, and Formstack compete on field types, templates, payments, workflows, integrations, exports, and compliance.
3. Modern builder experience tools: Typeform, Fillout, Tally, and Feathery compete on speed, design, logic, AI-assisted creation, high-conversion flows, and integrations with modern databases or SaaS stacks.
4. Survey/research platforms: SurveyMonkey and Qualtrics are not pure form tools; they win where research methodology, panels, analytics, and enterprise feedback programs matter.
5. WordPress plugins: Gravity Forms, WPForms, and Fluent Forms compete inside WordPress. Gravity Forms remains the best capability benchmark for WordPress-grade extensibility and professional workflows.

The main strategic opportunity for Geiger Forms is not "more form fields." The opportunity is to combine:

- The practical capability coverage of Gravity Forms.
- The fast, modern UX of Fillout/Tally/Typeform.
- The response-management depth that most tools lack: threads, audit logs, triage, revisions, task escalation, policy acknowledgements, and suite-native file/project handling.
- Pricing that makes advanced operations features accessible before users are forced into enterprise contracts.

## Feature Rarity Framework

Use this as the first scoring axis. A feature's rarity does not automatically make it valuable; it tells us how much differentiation it can create if it solves a real workflow.

### Category A: Table Stakes

These are expected in any serious form app. Missing them makes the product feel incomplete.

- Form builder with drag/drop or fast block-based editing.
- Core field types: text, email, phone, number, textarea, radio, checkbox, dropdown, date/time, file upload.
- Required fields, validation, placeholder/help text.
- Public share link and website embed.
- Basic themes and branding.
- Response list/detail view.
- CSV export.
- Email notifications to owner.
- Confirmation message after submission.
- Spam protection.
- Templates.
- Basic conditional logic.
- Multi-page or sectioned forms.
- Basic analytics: views, starts, submissions, completion rate.

### Category B: Common Paid Differentiators

These often sit behind paid plans and are useful for small businesses.

- Remove vendor branding.
- Custom domains or custom share links.
- Redirect after submit.
- Custom confirmation emails to respondents.
- Hidden fields and URL prefill.
- Webhooks and API access.
- Native integrations: Sheets, Slack, Zapier, Make, Airtable, Notion, HubSpot, Salesforce.
- Payments via Stripe/PayPal/Square.
- Signatures.
- Calculations and scoring.
- Save-and-resume or partial submissions.
- Custom PDFs.
- Team collaboration and basic roles.
- Response caps and scheduled close dates.
- Custom CSS.
- Google Analytics/Meta pixel tracking.

### Category C: Advanced But Increasingly Expected

These are not universal, but buyers increasingly expect them when forms become operational tools.

- Approval workflows.
- Conditional emails and conditional redirects.
- Appointment scheduling fields.
- Coupons, tax, subscriptions, invoices.
- Multi-step branching beyond simple show/hide logic.
- Drop-off analytics per question.
- Partial submission recovery.
- Offline mobile collection.
- HIPAA/GDPR/SOC 2 posture.
- SSO/SAML and enforced 2FA.
- Audit logs.
- Data retention settings.
- Per-form permissions.
- AI form generation.
- AI report insights or summarization.

### Category D: Rare High-Value Workflow Features

These create meaningful differentiation because most form tools still treat submissions like static spreadsheet rows.

- Response threads for internal review.
- Response-to-task escalation.
- Auto-triage and priority scoring.
- Named branching outcomes with separate analytics.
- Respondent edit links with revision history.
- Form revision history and restore.
- Smart field prefill from prior contact context, not just URL parameters.
- Policy acknowledgement mode with immutable version-pinned records.
- Delegate submission with attribution/audit trail.
- Counter-signature or attestation.
- Access logs per response.
- Sensitive field masking by context.
- Mandatory review windows.
- Duplicate detection with configurable policies.

### Category E: Extremely Rare / Enterprise-Grade Differentiators

These are expensive to build but can justify premium positioning if Geiger wants to own compliance-heavy workflows.

- Field-level encryption with workspace-controlled keys.
- True anonymisation mode that strips identity signals before storage.
- Per-workspace or per-form data residency.
- Signed personalised links with forwarding/identity mismatch detection.
- Offline sync with schema-change conflict handling.
- Immutable audit records for legal/compliance workflows.
- Bring-your-own-storage for uploads or exports.
- File uploads routed into an asset management system instead of generic storage.
- Knowledge graph/CRM entity creation from response mapping.
- Region-aware retention/deletion with audit evidence.

## Gravity Forms Capability Coverage Target

The user's instinct to cover Gravity Forms is sound, but the better target is "Gravity Forms capability coverage without becoming WordPress-shaped."

Minimum Gravity-class capability set:

- Visual builder.
- Conditional logic.
- Multi-page forms.
- Calculations.
- Payments.
- File uploads.
- Notifications and confirmations.
- Save/resume or partial completion.
- Entry management.
- Import/export.
- Add-on/integration model.
- Spam protection.
- Role/capability controls.
- Developer hooks or webhook/API surface.

Where Geiger should go beyond Gravity Forms:

- Response threads and review workflow.
- Versioned form publishing.
- First-class analytics and drop-off diagnosis.
- Suite-native task escalation.
- Asset-backed file uploads.
- Policy acknowledgement and audit modes.
- Modern, non-WordPress hosted/public form experience.

## Recommended Geiger Feature Strategy

### Baseline Relevance Pack

Build these first to be taken seriously against modern form builders:

- Conditional logic.
- Multi-step sections with progress.
- CSV/JSON export.
- Custom post-submit screen and redirects.
- Confirmation emails.
- Response caps and scheduling.
- Payments or at least payment-ready schema.
- File uploads.
- Basic templates.
- Basic analytics.

### Competitive Value Pack

These make Geiger feel like a modern business tool rather than a simple form clone:

- Field-level drop-off analytics.
- Respondent edit link with edit window.
- Form revision history.
- Calculated summary fields.
- Partial submissions.
- Hidden fields and smart prefill.
- Webhooks/API.
- Custom domains or clean branded URLs.

### Geiger Differentiation Pack

These should be the signature features:

- Response threads.
- Response-to-task escalation into Flow.
- Asset-backed file fields.
- Auto-triage/scoring queues.
- Named branching outcomes.
- Workspace role enforcement via Flow RBAC.
- Policy acknowledgement mode.
- Sensitive field masking and response access log.

### Enterprise Trust Pack

Do this when the core product is stable enough to sell into policy-driven organizations:

- Data retention/deletion policies.
- Field-level encryption.
- True anonymisation mode.
- SSO/enforced 2FA.
- Data residency.
- Delegated submissions.
- Counter-signature.
- Offline-ready forms.

## Pricing Relevance Hypothesis

Most competitors gate serious operational features too early:

- Tally competes with generosity: unlimited forms/submissions free.
- Fillout competes with a strong free plan and unlimited seats/forms.
- Jotform competes with breadth but adds usage limits.
- Typeform competes with experience but becomes expensive as response volume and automation needs grow.
- Formstack/FormAssembly compete with enterprise trust but feel expensive for small teams.
- Gravity Forms has attractive annual pricing but is limited to WordPress and often depends on add-ons.

Geiger can be relevant if it offers:

- A generous free/low-cost builder for basic forms.
- A paid tier that includes operational response management earlier than competitors do.
- Enterprise/compliance features as add-ons or higher tiers, not as the only path to serious workflows.

## Suggested Scoring Matrix

For the next pass, score every candidate feature with:

| Field | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Feature | The capability being evaluated |
| User value | Low / Medium / High / Critical |
| Market commonality | Table stakes / Common paid / Advanced / Rare / Extremely rare |
| Competitors offering it | Evidence from leading tools |
| Pricing gate | Free / Starter / Pro / Business / Enterprise |
| Implementation cost | Low / Medium / High |
| Geiger fit | Weak / Good / Strong / Signature |
| Risk | Security, privacy, UX, maintenance, or support risk |
| Recommended phase | Now / Next / Later / Avoid |
